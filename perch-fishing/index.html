🎣 White Perch Fishing Tips for Tuckahoe River

  • Location: Target ledges where channels meet flats (3-12 ft depth). Perch congregate where forage spills over drop-offs.
  • Best Technique: Anchor on a ledge, chum with grass shrimp to attract schools. Keep the slick going steadily.
  • Bait: Grass shrimp, bloodworms, or small jigs tipped with shrimp all make sense here.
  • Rig: High-low pill float rig with small hooks or light jigs depending on current.
  • Timing: High tide and the first part of the outgoing tide are usually the play here.
  • Water Temp: Warm spring afternoons help, especially after cooler nights.
  • Tidal Movement: Fish an hour before and after high tide when possible.
  • Chumming: A little shrimp goes a long way. Keep fish interested without overfeeding them.
  • Double Headers: When the bite is on, be ready for quick repeat bites.
  • Bag Limit: Follow current NJ regulations and verify before fishing.
