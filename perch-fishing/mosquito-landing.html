🎣 White Perch Fishing Tips for Tuckahoe River

  • Location: Target ledges where channels meet flats (3-12 ft depth). Perch congregate where forage spills over drop-offs.
  • Best Technique: Anchor on a ledge, chum with grass shrimp to attract schools. Keep slick going continuously!
  • Bait: Grass shrimp (live or preserved), bloodworms, or SPRO Phat Fly jigs tipped with shrimp.
  • Rig: High-low pill float rig with size 4-6 hooks, 1 oz bank sinker. Or 1/16-1/8 oz jigs.
  • Timing: Outgoing tide in late morning/afternoon is prime - sun warms shallows and draws perch onto flats.
  • Water Temp: Perch active 40-48°F. Sunny days after cold spells are excellent.
  • Tidal Movement: Fish 1 hour before and after high tide for best action.
  • Chumming: Essential! Toss handful of grass shrimp every minute. Add Pro-Cure Shrimp Oil for scent.
  • Double Headers: When bite is on, perch come up two at a time - have multiple rods ready!
  • Bag Limit: 25 fish per person in NJ saltwater. Plan ~3 fish per diner for meals.
