🎣 White Perch Fishing Tips for Dividing Creek

  • Location: Target feeder creeks, marsh drains, outside bends, and deeper holes where bait gets funneled into holding lanes.
  • Best Technique: Anchor or drift creek edges and let light chum bring fish to you.
  • Bait: Grass shrimp, bloodworms, or small jigs tipped with shrimp are the confidence baits here.
  • Rig: High-low rig, pill float rig, or small jig under a float if bottom is soft or grassy.
  • Timing: High tide into the first part of the outgoing push is usually the key feeding window.
  • Water Type: Brackish tidal creek water is classic South Jersey white perch habitat.
  • Tidal Movement: Fish 1 hour before and after high tide, then follow the first outgoing movement near drains and bends.
  • Chumming: A little grass shrimp goes a long way. Enough to gather fish, not enough to overfeed them.
  • Current Seams: Focus where fast water meets soft water, especially near side drains.
  • Wind: If the wind gets ugly, hide on protected bends and fish smaller areas carefully.
