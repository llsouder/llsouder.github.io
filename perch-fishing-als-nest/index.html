🎣 White Perch Tips for Owls Nest / Hanseys Creek

  • Location: Target feeder creeks, marsh drains, outside bends, and creek junctions where Dividing Creek water movement pushes bait into small holding lanes.
  • Best Technique: Anchor or drift near bends and drain mouths, then chum lightly with grass shrimp to hold a school in place.
  • Bait: Grass shrimp, bloodworms, or small jigs tipped with shrimp are the main confidence baits here.
  • Rig: High-low rig, pill float rig, or small jig under a float if the bottom is soft or grassy.
  • Timing: In this area, high tide into the first outgoing push is usually the key feeding window, especially where water drains off marsh edges into the main creek.
  • Water Type: Brackish tidal creek water is classic South Jersey white perch habitat.
  • Current Seams: Focus on the seam where fast water meets slow water, especially near drain mouths and outside bends.
  • Wind: If the wind gets ugly, hide on protected bends and fish smaller areas more carefully.
  • Schooling Fish: Once you locate one productive zone, the bite can get fast in a hurry.
  • Compare Days: Monday is the best immediate setup, Tuesday next, and the bigger April new-moon window is still the real prize.
